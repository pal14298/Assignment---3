Prey & Predators

Muskies are apex predators, feeding on a variety of prey, including fish, frogs, birds, and small mammals. Their diet primarily consists of other fish, such as perch, sunfish, and even other Muskies. They are ambush predators, using their camouflage and swift attacks to capture prey.

Despite being top predators, young Muskies can fall prey to larger fish, birds, and even adult Muskies. As they grow, they become less vulnerable, with their only significant threats being humans and large birds of prey.
